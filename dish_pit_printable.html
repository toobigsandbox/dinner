The Dish Pit Loop

Three sinks. One rhythm. You keep the kitchen flowing.

Step 1 BEFORE THE SINKS
Dirty plate
Scrape
Stacked

Scrape & Sort

  • Scrape food into the trash first
  • Stack like with like — pans together, utensils in a bin
  • Heavy pans go last — they don't block the line

"Left to right. Dirty to clean."

Step 2 · Sink 1 WATER LINE
WASH · WATER LINE
DAWN
Hot + Soap

Wash

  • Hot soapy water — as hot as you can handle
  • Scrub until all food is gone
  • Gloves available — just ask

Hot water = slow hands. No rushing.

Step 3 · Sink 2 WATER LINE
RINSE · WATER LINE
Quick dip

Rinse

  • Clear water only — no soap in this sink
  • Quick full dip to remove all soap residue
  • No scrubbing — move right on through

"In, dip, out. Keep moving."

Step 4 · Sink 3 WATER LINE
Full Dunk
SANITIZE · WATER LINE
60 sec
Hold & Wait

Sanitize

  • Full submerge — every surface under the water
  • Hold for 60 seconds — use the timer
  • Test strips are on the wall — ask the chef if unsure

This step protects the guests. Worth every second.

Step 5 CART · AIR DRY
Roll Away
No Towel

Rack on the Cart

  • Slide dishes into the blue racks on the cart
  • Roll cart a safe distance away from the pit
  • Air dry only — towels reintroduce bacteria

"Rack it, roll it, let it breathe."

Reset the Station

  • Refill soap when it gets low
  • Dump rinse water when it turns cloudy
  • Check sanitizer with test strips — ask the chef if unsure

"Now the loop starts again. You are the engine."

Sensory & Advocacy Supports

If the heat is too much

Step back. Shake out your hands. Rejoin the loop when ready.

If it's too loud or overwhelming

Earplugs are available. Say "In the weeds" and someone will help.

Wet floor safety

Shuffle your feet. No fast moves. Heavy pans = two hands, always.
